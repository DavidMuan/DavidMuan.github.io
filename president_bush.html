President Bush

"Either you are with us, or you are with the terrorists" - President Bush

George W. Bush, became the 41st president of the United States, was born in 1924 in Massachusetts. He served as a Navy pilot in World War II. After the war, he graduated from Yale. Then he went into the oil business in Texas - he had a long political career as a congressman. He was an ambassador to the United Nations. He also became director of the CIA. Before his presidency, he served as vice president under Ronald Reagan. In 1989, he became president. The Gulf War, the fall of the Berlin Wall, and the end of the Cold War were important events during his time as president. But he lost reelection in 1992. His son, George W. Became the 43rd president. Born in 1946, he grew up in Texas; he also graduated from Yale. From Harvard, he earned an MBA. He then worked in the oil industry. He also owned a part of the Texas Rangers baseball team. In 1994, he won election as governor of Texas - he became president in 2001. His presidency was shaped by the September 11 attacks. The War on Terror, the wars in Afghanistan but also Iraq, and domestic ideas such as tax cuts and education changes were also part of his presidency. He served two terms.
