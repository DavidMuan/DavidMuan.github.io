President Washington

"The harder the conflict, the greater the triumph." - President Washington

George Washington, the first President of the United States, was born on February 22, 1732, in Westmoreland County, Virginia. During his early age, Washington became a surveyor then later served as a military officer in both the French and Indian War. Later became the Commander-in-Chief, appointed by the Continental Congress, He led the colonies to war against the Great Britian during the American Revolution. Washington remained the president after the war in the year 1787 and in 1789, became the first elected President, where he served two terms. During those time, he set precedents for the office. Washington declined a third term and returned to his home, Mount Vernon. He later became known as the "Father of the Country" and is still to this day, a respected figure in America History. Washington passed away on December 14, 1799.
